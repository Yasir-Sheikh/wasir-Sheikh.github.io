Misfortunes in David Henry’s life is used to create secrecy to support the theme. One way this is shown in the novel is through David’s misfortune of losing his sister to Down Syndrome and the effect this misfortune has on his future actions. For instance, David realizes his daughter, Phoebe, has Down Syndrome; this reminds him of his misfortune as shown by, “He thought of his sister, pale, thin, trying to catch her breath, and his mother turning to the window to hide her tears.”(Edwards 18). With the remembrance of his pain, he gives away his daughter shown by, “‘There’s a place,’[...] ‘I’d like to you to take her there.[...]’”(18). David’s loss of his sister left him in pain and grief. When he faces a similar situation with his daughter, his misfortune impacts his actions. This is observed when he gives away Phoebe to save himself and his family from the pain and grief that he had suffered. He made this decision without the knowledge of his family and is never able to open up about the secret. This shows how David’s misfortune leads to secrecy. This reinforces the theme because David becomes more distant from his family after the secret. Many of David’s emotions are caused by the secret but his family is unable to understand this. Without him opening up about the secret, his family becomes more distant from him as he starts becoming more like a stranger to them. This leads to the downfall and dismay of his family. Another misfortune in David’s life is him giving away his daughter which leads him to become a photographer to cope with his guilt. When David is explaining his love for photography to Paul, he says,“‘ Photography is all about secrets’[....] ‘The secrets we all have and will never tell’”(201). When David passes away, his wife, Norah, finds a photo album which is explained by, “[...] all those years of silence, when he would not speak of their lost daughter, David had been keeping this record of her absence. Paul, and a thousand other girls, all growing.”(364). At the time when David gives Phoebe away, he feels that he made the right decision. With time, David begins feeling guilty about his decision. David’s guilt leads to his obsession with photography. He did photography to cope for his decisions in the past as he felt that the photos he took held secrets and meanings that only he would understand. He took photos of multiple young girls aging and growing. This was David’s way of experiencing Phoebe growing up. This idea supports the theme as the secrets held within David’s photography was something his family didn’t understand. This leads to the family becoming more distant as they feel that David doesn't care for them and only cares for his photography. In addition, David lying about the death of his daughter causes a difficulty that leads to a secret between him and Rosemary, a pregnant girl found at David’s old house. His feelings about the lie he has told Norah are expressed by, “[...] he had woken up every morning of his life for eighteen years thinking maybe today, maybe this was the day he would put things right.”(274). His reasonings for sympathizing with Rosemary are shown by, “It was more, he suspected, that Rosemary knew his secret. He’d poured his story out to her in such a rush, the first and last time he had ever told it and she had listened to what he'd done without judging him. There was freedom in that; David could be completely himself with Rosemary”(311). David’s misfortune of telling a lie to Norah leads to him feeling guilty and responsible for everything wrong in their relationship. This results in David telling a stranger, Rosemary, about the lie. He sympathizes with Rosemary as she is the only one who can truly understand David. This supports the theme as David becoming attached to a stranger distances him from his family. His family is unable to understand the relationship between Rosemary and David resulting in unanswered questions for the rest of the family. This eventually results in the divorce of David and Norah, where Norah lives with Paul and David lives with Rosemary. This shows the downfall of David’s family caused by the secrecy and mystery between Rosemary and David’s relationship. In summary, David’s misfortunes all resulted in varying secrets which supports the theme of a life lived in secrecy and mystery leads to the downfall and dismay of close relations of the family.Misfortunes in David Henry’s life is used to create secrecy to support the theme.
